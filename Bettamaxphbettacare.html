BETTA CARE

A happy betta is a healthy betta

BASIC INFORMATION

Betta splendens (Regan, 1910) originated from the Malaysian word "ikan betah" that is the species' name; splendens - a Latin word that is equated into 'shining or glittering'. These species grow from 60 - 70mm in size.
Order: Perciformes
Family Osphronemidae 
Bettas can live up to 7 years with proper care and maintenance. However, some bettas only live up to 2-3 years depending on various circumstances.

DIET

These fishes eat marine and terrestrial organisms in their environment. However those in captivity can be fed with edible food such as pellets, grindal worms, daphnia, brine shrimp, bloodworms and tubifex to noursish and improve their color. Some also use mosquito larvae as substitute since it is pretty prevalent in the Philippines due to its tropical climate.

Feeding should be regulated especially during cold seasons since their metabolism is slower during those times. It is advisable to feed them 1-2 pellets or organisms. Overfeeding should be kept on watch to prevent them from death. .

HABITAT

These betta fishes are commonly distributed in countries such as central and peninsular Thailand and the portion of Mekong River basin that covers eastern Thailand, Laos, Cambodia and Vietnam. In these countries, these fishes occupy motionless waters that are inundated and have substrates which differ from mud and others.

In the Philippines, the emergence of betta keeping and spawning has been part of the growing hobby and often are imported from Thailand to be bred and cultured..

TANK

Housing a betta is a very particular factor in betta keeping. Prior purchasing a betta, the tank should be prioritized first. A tank should be 5g at minimum for regular sized bettas and 10g above for giant or monster bettas. A small tank decreases the betta's life expectancy rate. Avoid using bowls as a tank, there is a big tendency for the betta to be dizzy since the path is circular and later on weakens the fish. Choosing a substrate, whether soil,pebbles or gravel sand, it should be 2 inches to cushion the plant's roots. Afterwards, pick a plant suitable for your betta. Use live plants as much as possible to avoid fin damage. 

TANK MATES

Due to a betta's territorial aggresion,  creating a community tank should be thought of critically. Also, cohabitants which are incompatible with bettas can cause stress. Male bettas can't be joined together in one tank unless separated by a divider. Females can interact with each other in a single tank as long as they have been exposed to each and everyone's existence since birth. Males and females should not be placed in the same tank unless for spawning purposes. However, during the spawning period the female should be introduced slowly to the male by placing it in a small container in the tank before joining them. Tank mates are not required for the maintenance of bettas yet keepers place these beings in order to liven up the tank. Some tank mates are also utilized to contribute to the nitrogen cycle and eliminate ammonia. If one is planning to put tankmates with bettas, it is better to place the betta in the established community tank. Before placing the new mates, acclimatize them in a separate tank.

BEHAVIOUR

Hostility of bettas is part of their genetics . However, constant aggression with whatever reason can initiate stress which can cause sickness or even death. Regulate flaring sessions and avoid betta fights. Bettas have a special organ for being breathing keep the surface clear of obstruction and place minimal amount of plants that float along the surface. Place a lid for your tank since there are instances that bettas jump out of the tank and insects or other organisms infest the tank that can affect the betta and the environment as well..

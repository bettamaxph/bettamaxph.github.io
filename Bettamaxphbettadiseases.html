BETTA DISEASES

Mouth Fungus

Unmaintained tank can cause a bacteria Columnaris to infest the environment which enters lacerations in bettas. It is spotted to be stringy cottony patches along the mouth, gills and fins which can lead to fin damage and lesions.

Symptoms: White cottony patches along the body and/or gills

Treatment: Clean water and anti-fungus treatment, hard talisay session with a pinch of rock salt.

Dropsy

Normally known as pine coning. This is caused by a bacteria that infested the betta's  internal organs. Most who suffer this ailment are always close to the surface. Since the body swells which makes them struggle in breathing. Visible swells occur in the abdomen and the scales flare outwards.

Symptoms: Severe body swelling and outward scaling 

Treatment: Kanamycin Sulfate or
Maracyn II. This disease cannot be mediated since it is leading to death. It is recommended for keepers who have bettas suffering from dropsy to provide everything that is necessary for the betta to enjoy its stay in their environment to lessen their suffering. However, some resort to euthansia. 

Fin rot

Based on many experts, this is the most common betta disease. The caudal fin ,tail, that are red or black tattered is visible. Symptoms include fin tears which should be kept on watch. If not treated, it can lead to body rot.

Symptoms: Black or red tattering rot in their tails and/or edges

Treatment:. Clean water, hard talisay treatment with aquarium salt. Some use antibiotics such as Kanaplex and Maracyn II for extreme cases. 

Hole in the Head

This is caused by inappropriate feeding and habitat cleanliness. It is mainly accompanied by sores or dents along the head or above the eyes. If not cured it can run along the head and be deadly.

Symptoms: Visible holes along the head or above the eyes

Treatment: Clean water, talisay treatment and proper feeding.

Ick

Mainly caused by parasites that develop small white dots all over the betta's body.

Symptoms: Small white dots all over the body, bumping or  scratching among tank areas, decors or plants

Treatment: Frequent water change, clean water, Rid Ich Plus or Mardel Copper Safe.

Popeye

This characterized by outward bulging of the betta's eyes which can cause long term damage such as blindness. It is also the first symptom for betta tuberculosis.

Symptoms: Bulging eye

Treatment:. Clean water, talisay treatment, antibiotics such as Kanaplex and Maracyn II.

Septicemia

This is caused by high nitrite rates in the tank  and bacterial infection which develops red streaks in the betta's body. If left untreated, it can develop into dropsy and organ failure.

Symptoms: Red streaks or spots under scales, ulcers, light coloration, appetite loss, lethargy and clamped fins.

Treatment: Use antibiotics for gram-positive and gram-negative bacterias such as Metronidazole .

Swim bladder disorder

This can be detected by seeing difficulty in breathing, s-shaped spine structure, inability to swim towards the surface, always located in the bottom of the tank. Bloating is caused by overfeeding and constipation which leads to the abovementioned .

Symptoms: Floating on one side, difficulty swimming, vertical swimming
 
Treatment: Fasting for 2-3 days, Pea feeding for constipation.

Velvet

This is characterized by rust sprinkle-colored parasite that reside on the betta's gills, body, and fins. Due to iching there is an urge for bettas to scratch off these parasites anywhere in their environment. Bumping and lesions can cause organ failure and worse, death.

Symptoms: Appearance of rust-colored sprinkes along the betta's gills, body and fins, difficulty in breathing, clamped fins and scratching
 
Treatment: Clean water, do a 100% water change, remove the light source to weaken the parasite, increase the water temperature by 76- 81 degrees, use copper sulfate to treat the disease.

TYPES OF BETTA

COMMON BETTAS

Veiltail

Most popular type of common betta. This is often characterized by a long and almost v-shaped caudal fin. Beginners often start with this kind of betta due to its behavior and it is readily availble in the market since it is cheap.

Crowntail

Often characterized with spiky tail resembling a crown. The tail is webbing beween rays that is also one of the breeds of crowntail.

Delta or Super Delta

Their tails have a straight shape and don't cover the 180 angle unlike halfmoon bettas.

Doubletail

It has two distinct tails  which are shaped like a half moon. This is a different variation which has a double split instead of only a singular split.

Combtail

This is a result of breeding a veiltail and a crowntail. It is differentiated from the crowntail since it shows a less spiky tail.

Halfmoon

These is the type of betta that is often the apple of the eye of the keepers since its fins cover the 180 angle which is also shaped like 'D'. 

Plakat

These bettas are smaller than the usual size and are said to be the ancestors of all bettas. Yet their fins are shorter and rounder and in relation to this, they more aggresive in behavior.

Rosetail

This is a type of halfmoon bettas which is resulted from exaggeration of branches and overlapping of fin rays.

Fantail

It has only a round single full tail.

ALIEN BETTAS

BETTA BELLICA

Origin: Malaysia

Description: One of the largest and thickest of the betta breed, short finned, heart shaped back fin

BETTA PICTA

Origin: Southeast Asia

Description: Easiest betta to breed, lacks vibrant colors, the least aggressive among the betta family

BETTA SMARAGDINA

Origin: Northeast Thailand, Upper Mekong watershed, Mun basin, Khorat highlands and plateaus

Description: Has smaller heads, shorter fins and slimmer bodies compared with Siamese betta fighting fish,shows common signs of aggression, rare and pure blooded

BETTA TAENIATA

Origin: Borneo

Description: When used as a pet, these kinds enjoy low light conditions and isolated areas

BETTA SIMPLEX

Origin: Krabi, Thailand

Description: Do no grow longer than 2 inches, great jumpers due to their small size, great choice for community tanks since they are peaceful beings, female betta simplex breeds the male to death

BETTA FALX

Origin: Jambi, Indonesia

Description: can be kept in community type as they are gentle and small, they need proper water acidity as to replicate their normal habitat. Betta Falx can change its color depending on its mood

BETTA PRIMA

Origin: Thailand and Cambodia

Description: Distinct marking of 3 horizontal lines on their body, can be kept in pairs or in community type tanks, more inclined to a carnivourous diet

BETTA EDITHAE

Origin: Kalimantan (South Borneo)

Description: also known as Pseudo Betta, enjoys being paired and placed in community tanks.

BETA CHANNOIDES

Origin: Kalimantan Timur, Indonesia

Description: also known as Snakehead Betta or Snakehead FIghter, stunning adult coloration Very similar to Betta albimarginata, though Betta channoides are smaller and have rounder caudal fin

BETTA IMBELLIS

Origin: Native to Malaysia and Indonesia

Description: also known as the Peaceful Betta or Crescent Betta, great fish for community tanks, even males can be kept together, Cover is alo needed as to prevent it from jumping out, Betta imbellis are jumpers

BETTA IBANORUM

Origin: southern & western Sarawak, Borneo

Description: it can be found from blackwater peat swamp to clearwater mountain stream

BETTA KRATAIOS

Origin: Kapuas River basin at Kalimantan Barat in the Heart of Borneo.

 a distinctive iridescent blue operculum, and more predorsal and lateral scales than other members of the group, as well as lacking the extended fin rays seen in some other species in the complex.  

BETTA MACROSTOMA

Origin: Brunei, Malaysia and Sarawak

Description: Also known as Spotfin Betta These fish are meant to be kept as a pair provided they are kept in a min 40 gallon tank in order to accomidate his own and her own space and also because this fish can swim extremely fast, This breed has been known to kill themselves by swimming against the tank glass. Tanks should be covered extremely well, as Betta macrostoma is also an excellent jumper.

BETTA KUEHNEI

Origin: Malay Peninsula

Description: They are found in streams that are strongly shaded by forest, with clear, colourless water. Found in shallow water between water plants and leaves.

BETA TUSSYAE

Origin: Malaysia

Description: Deep red coloration changes the betta's mood, a female tussyae has lighter coloration than the male, and a horizontal stripe can be observed in some of them.

BETTA CHLOROPHARYNX 

Origin: Banka, Indonesia

Description: Also known as Green Throat Mouth. It is a betta type that can grow up to 6 inches in their adulthood. They share the same behavior with betta splendens

BETTA PI

Origin: Pru-Toe-Daeng peat swamps

Description: 
This kind of species are at risk due to industrial transformation of their habitat

BETTA RAJA

Origin: Sumatra, Indonesia

Description: 
These bettas can be grouped together, even males, Males have a flatter head from a front view than females and have a spiky caudal fin.

BETTA PATOTI

Origin: Borneo and Palau Luat Island, Seasia

Description: Also known as a tiger betta. It is a peach colored betta which is large in size which reach the size of 5 inches. These are great in community tanks but are very difficult to find for the hobby.

BETTA FOERSCHI

Origin: Indonesia and South Borneo

Description: Males are thinner and have vibrant colors than females. The gill cover bands are differentiated for females and males to distinguish their sex: females are red while males are gold.

BETTA BALUNGA

Origin: Northeast Borneo

Description:  This further classified into Betta akarensis, Betta climacura, Betta chini and Betta balunga.
